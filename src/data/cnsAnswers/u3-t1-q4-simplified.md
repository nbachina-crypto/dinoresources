A **prime number** is a number greater than `1` that can be divided only by:
* `1`
* Itself
---
## Quick Examples
* **Prime:** `2, 3, 5, 7`
* **Not prime:** `4, 6, 8`
---
## Primality Testing (Simplified)
* **Trial Division:** Check divisibility from `2` to `√n`
* **Fermat Test:** Check if `a^(n−1) mod n = 1`
* **Miller–Rabin:** Advanced probabilistic test
* **Sieve Method:** Find all primes up to a limit
---
> **Key Idea:** Prime numbers are the building blocks of integers and play a critical role in **number theory** and **cryptographic systems**.
