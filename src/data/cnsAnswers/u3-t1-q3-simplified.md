Modular arithmetic deals with **remainders after division**.
```text
a ≡ b (mod n)
```
means both numbers give the **same remainder** when divided by `n`.
---
## Quick Examples
### Example 1
```text
17 mod 5 = 2
```
So:
* `17 ≡ 2 (mod 5)`
---
### Example 2
```text
(7 + 9) mod 5 = 1
```
---
## Key Properties
* Addition and multiplication behave normally (**commutative**, **associative**)
* **Distributive law** holds
* Every number has an **additive inverse**
* **Multiplicative inverse** exists only if numbers are **coprime**
---
> **Key Idea:** Modular arithmetic keeps numbers within a fixed range `{0, 1, ..., n−1}`, making it extremely useful in **number theory** and **cryptographic systems**.
