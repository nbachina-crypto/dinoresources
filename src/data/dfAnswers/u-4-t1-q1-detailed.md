**Embedded systems, mobile phones, and telecommunication networks** are important sources of **digital evidence** in forensic investigations because they **store, process, and transmit digital data** related to user activities and communications.
---
## Embedded Systems
**Embedded systems** are specialized computing devices built into larger systems to perform dedicated functions.
Examples include:
* **ATMs**
* **smart TVs**
* **routers**
* **CCTV systems**
* **GPS devices**
* **smart appliances**
* **vehicle control units**
In digital forensics, embedded system analysis focuses on extracting:
* **stored data**
* **configuration logs**
* **event records**
* **timestamps**
* **device activity history**
Evidence may include:
* **log files**
* **firmware data**
* **access records**
* **usage timelines**
### Examination Process
```text id="64128"
identify device
acquire storage image
extract logs and configuration data
analyze timestamps
reconstruct activity timeline
```
These systems are important because they often record **operational events and user interactions**.
> **Embedded devices often provide timeline evidence through event logs and timestamps.**
---
## Mobile Phones
**Mobile phone forensics** deals with the recovery and analysis of data from **smartphones and feature phones**.
### Important Evidence Sources
* **call logs**
* **SMS and chat messages**
* **contacts**
* **photos and videos**
* **browser history**
* **app data**
* **location records**
* **deleted files**
Investigators create a **forensic copy** of the mobile storage and **SIM-related data** using specialized tools.
Analysis may include:
* **internal memory**
* **external SD cards**
* **application databases**
Mobile devices are significant because they provide **personal communication history** and **timeline evidence**.
---
## Telecommunication Networks
**Telecommunication network forensics** focuses on communication data transmitted through network infrastructure such as:
* **cellular towers**
* **switches**
* **service provider logs**
### Common Evidence Sources
* **call detail records (CDR)**
* **SMS routing logs**
* **IP session logs**
* **tower location data**
* **time and duration records**
These records help investigators:
* **trace communication between parties**
* **identify call times**
* **verify user location**
* **reconstruct communication timelines**
This is especially useful in:
* **criminal investigations**
* **fraud cases**
* **incident timeline reconstruction**
