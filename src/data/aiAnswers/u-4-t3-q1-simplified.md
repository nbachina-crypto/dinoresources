**Propositional Inference** works with simple statements like:
P → Q
It treats each statement as a **whole unit**.
**First-Order Inference** works with objects and relationships like:
Human(x) → Mortal(x)
It uses **variables and quantifiers**.
### Main Difference
* **propositional** = simple true/false statements
* **first-order** = detailed logic with objects and relations
So, **first-order inference is more expressive and powerful than propositional inference**.
